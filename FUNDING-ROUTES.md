# IRLid — Funding & Revenue Routes

**Drafted by Number One, 11 June 2026.** Researched against current (June 2026) programme states.
Companion to `PROMOTION.md`. This is honest triage, not a wish-list — each route carries its
real eligibility gate and its real speed.

---

## The one thing to hold in your head

There are **two different problems** wearing the same word "money", and conflating them is the trap:

1. **Prove-it money (now).** Cover the Max plan, show the time isn't wasted, get *some* revenue on
   the board this quarter. → This comes from **the product and a loan**, NOT grants.
2. **Mission money (later).** Fund the humanitarian half — Wisdom's drone proof-of-delivery, scaling.
   → This is where the **grants and accelerators** live. They are slow (3–5 months) and competitive
   (~10–15% hit rate). Worth pursuing, but never as this month's rent.

Most founders burn out chasing slow grants to solve a fast problem. Don't.

## FAST — money in weeks, mostly in your control

### A. Org portal as a paid product (the strongest near-term play)
You now have something genuinely sellable: a check-in/out system with calendar, per-event expected
lists, five role tiers, doorman escalation, offline operation, and — as of today — co-presence-proofed
admin appointment. Gyms, studios, clubs, co-working spaces, classes all have this exact need and pay
monthly for worse tools (Glofox, TeamUp, Mindbody).
- **Action:** one paying pilot. Pick a local studio/gym/club, offer the Imbue-style portal free for a
  month then £X/month. One real customer paying anything changes the story from "hobby" to "business".
- **Why it's first:** it's the most defensible "I'm not wasting time" evidence — recurring revenue from
  a real user beats any grant letter.

### B. The verification widget (reCAPTCHA-for-presence)
`demo-login.html`'s "prove a human is physically here" gate is a real commercial category. Sell it as a
drop-in for event ticketing, second-hand marketplace meetups, dating safety check-ins.
- **Action:** package `WIDGET.md` into a one-page "add presence verification in 5 minutes" pitch.

### C. Patreon, reactivated
Already live with supporters. A "v6 is real, here's a Lead Admin seated on cryptographic co-presence"
update post + a clear tier ask is low effort and immediate.

## MEDIUM — money in months, debt or competitive

### D. UK Start Up Loan (government-backed, British Business Bank) — most accessible
- **Up to £25,000** for a solo founder, **7.5% fixed**, repay over 1–5 years, no fees, **+12 months free
  mentoring**. As of 6 April 2026 eligibility widened to businesses **trading up to 5 years**.
- Assessed on **you + a business plan**, not trading history — fits a solo dev with a working product.
- **Honest caveat:** it's *debt*. It adds repayment pressure. Take it for a specific growth purpose
  (hardware for the Wisdom pilot, a designer, runway to land paying customers) — not to plug the Max bill.
- **startuploans.co.uk**

### E. Innovate UK Smart Grants
- Open to **any UK-registered business, any sector, any age**; quarterly rounds; values novelty +
  disruptive potential — IRLid's protocol genuinely qualifies on the "technological uncertainty" test
  (ECDSA co-presence, ZK roadmap).
- **Reality:** 3–5 months to decision, ~10–15% success, needs a credible *commercial exploitation* plan
  and usually a project framing (not just "fund my existing thing"). Strong fit for a written bid; bring
  in Mr. Data/Troi to draft, you to steer.
- **apply-for-innovation-funding.service.gov.uk**

### F. R&D tax credits — only once you're a limited company
- Software R&D with real technological uncertainty (your crypto work qualifies; routine CRUD doesn't).
  Worth roughly **15–27p back per £1** of qualifying spend (dev time, cloud/Cloudflare costs).
- **Gate:** this is a **Corporation Tax** relief — it needs **IRLid to be a Ltd company** paying corp
  tax. If you're a sole trader today, it does nothing *yet*. Flag for the moment you incorporate; then
  it retroactively rewards the dev hours you're already pouring in.

## SLOW — mission money, the humanitarian route

### G. WFP Innovation Accelerator — best humanitarian fit ⭐
- Explicitly seeking **track & trace, supply-chain resilience, emergency-response logistics** — that is
  *proof-of-delivery*, exactly the Wisdom/ASE Tech drone angle. **Non-dilutive seed funding** + pilots
  across 120+ countries + mentoring. **Rolling applications.**
- **This is the one to lead with** — and the natural vehicle is the Wisdom partnership (a humanitarian
  operator + your accountability protocol is a far stronger application than either alone).
- **innovation.wfp.org/apply**

### H. Elrha — Humanitarian Innovation Fund (HIF)
- UK-based funder of humanitarian innovation; periodic themed calls (data-driven inclusion, GBV tech,
  WASH, scaling). Check live calls; the "tamper-evident accountability for last-mile aid" framing fits
  several themes. **elrha.org/funding-opportunities**

### I. UNICEF Venture Fund — likely NOT eligible (be honest)
- Up to $100k equity-free, but: this cycle is **blockchain-specific**, the deadline was **10 March 2026
  (passed)**, and crucially applicants must be **registered in a UNICEF programme country** — the **UK is
  not one**. So IRLid as a UK entity is **ineligible** as it stands. Only relevant if delivered *through*
  a programme-country partner (e.g. Wisdom's African registration). Park unless that route opens.

## Recommended order of attack

1. **This week:** one paying pilot conversation (A) + reactivate Patreon (C). Cheap, fast, story-changing.
2. **This month:** scope the Start Up Loan (D) for a *specific* growth purpose — and draft the WFP
   application (G) jointly with Wisdom.
3. **This quarter:** Innovate UK Smart Grant bid (E), drafted by the crew. Elrha (H) if a fitting call is open.
4. **When you incorporate:** R&D tax credits (F) start paying back the work already done.
5. **Skip for now:** UNICEF Venture Fund (I) unless the Wisdom programme-country route makes it viable.

The honest north star: **one paying customer + the WFP/Wisdom application in flight** is the combination
that proves this isn't wasted — recurring revenue answers the money question, the humanitarian bid answers
the "where it's heading" question. The grants are the upside, not the lifeline.
