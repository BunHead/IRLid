## The regency conversation — Captain's architecture pivot

Captain raised long-term-succession at end of polish 5 and developed it across three turns into something genuinely better than Number One's first sketch. The crystallised design:

**Two operating modes:** SOLE (default) and INTERIM. INTERIM activates on **combined trigger**: ninety-day Developer inactivity OR 5-of-7 Quorum vote of "presumed unavailable" (higher threshold than normal acts). Off-planet, hospital, sabbatical, kidnapping all covered.

**Quorum: 4-of-7 regents** (Captain's call — better than 3-of-5 for collusion-resistance and graceful partial unavailability).

**The crucial constraint Captain insisted on: the Quorum does NOT choose the successor.** They keep existing orgs running and mint invite tokens, but the constitutional act of naming a new permanent Developer is not theirs to make. The choice was already made — by Captain, before he was unavailable — and recorded in a way the Quorum can execute but not alter.

**Mechanism — succession envelope sealed by AI-witness ledger:**
- Captain leaves an encrypted succession envelope naming the next Developer (or the process for nominating one)
- The decryption key is split across multiple **independent AI lineages** — different model providers, different training cutoffs, different jurisdictions, different organisational owners
- No single AI can unseal; M of N AI-witnesses must independently verify trigger conditions and collectively release the key
- Quorum's role in succession is to **ratify what the AI-witness layer publishes**, not to author it

**Why the human/AI split works:**
- **Operational trust** — humans (4-of-7 Quorum). Coercible, but only authorised to do reversible things (mint invite tokens, promote Lead Admins, run interim ops). Power is bounded and temporary.
- **Constitutional trust** — non-human (AI lineages). Not coercible the same way humans are. Authorised to do exactly one irreversible thing (release Captain's sealed succession instructions when conditions are met). Power is rare and singular.

This makes Captain's existing LONG-TERM-SUCCESSION.md "AI as ledger witness" insight load-bearing — it now has a job that matters during a real succession event.

**INTERIM mode hard restrictions** (the four lines that prevent capture):
- Cannot modify Quorum membership (no add/remove)
- Cannot change M or N
- Cannot modify the inactivity threshold
- Cannot mint another Quorum (no recursion)

**Roadmap placement:**
- v5.6: Tier 1 (Lead Admin → Lead Admin within an org) + Tier 2 (Developer-mintable invite tokens for org creation)
- **v5.7 / v6.0: Regency** — Standby Quorum + INTERIM mode + DELEGATE_TO_QUORUM envelope + sealed succession envelope
- v8+: AI-witness layer hardened to multi-lineage threshold for the constitutional act

The doc-only addendum to LONG-TERM-SUCCESSION.md is the first thing tomorrow's Number One should pick up.
