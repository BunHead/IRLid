**Title:** Welcome to IRLid Discussions 👋

**Body:**

Hello, and welcome.

**IRLid** lets two people — or a venue and an attendee — prove they were really in the same place at the
same time: a cryptographically signed receipt of co-presence. No app to install, no biometrics by default,
no central authority. It's open source and built in the open, and this is the place to talk about it.

A few things this forum is for:

- **Questions** — using it, running check-ins at a venue, embedding the widget, anything at all.
- **Ideas & use cases** — where would proof-of-presence actually help you? We've heard everything from gym
  and class check-ins to humanitarian last-mile delivery.
- **Poke holes in the protocol** — genuinely, please. The honest threat model lives in `THREAT-MODEL.md`
  and the spec in `PROTOCOL.md`; we'd far rather you find the gaps here than in the wild. Good-faith
  adversarial thinking is the highest compliment you can pay this project.
- **Show us** — deployed it somewhere? We'd love to see it.

A few links to start:

- The tool: https://irlid.co.uk
- How it works, no jargon: https://irlid.co.uk/blog-proof-of-personhood.html
- Protocol spec + threat model: in the repo
- Support development: https://patreon.com/c/IRLid

If you like, **introduce yourself** in the comments — who you are, what brought you here, and what you'd
use proof-of-presence for. There are no wrong answers.

— Spencer
