## What IRLid is (one breath)
A browser-based way to **prove two people (or a person and a place) were genuinely together, in person,
at a moment in time** — no app, no central authority. Two parties scan each other's QR codes; the result
is a cryptographically **signed, tamper-evident receipt** of that co-presence. Live at **irlid.co.uk**.
Under the hood: ECDSA P-256 / WebAuthn, GPS + timestamp checks, hardware-backed device signing.

## What v6 is — the story to tell
IRLid grew up. It began as a tool for **two individuals** to prove they met. **v6 turns that same
cryptographic co-presence into a full check-in / check-out system any organisation can run** — events,
venues, gyms, studios, clinics, reception desks, the lot. What shipped:
- **Organisation portal** — run an event/venue, see live attendance (checked-in / checked-out, scan
  counts, scores) on a dashboard, with an airport-board "audit" view you can prop at a desk.
- **Calendar + per-event attendance** — multiple rooms, per-event expected lists, "who's here now".
- **Doorman flow** — a guest holds up their phone, staff scans it; nobody hands a device over.
- **Roles** — attendee / staff / manager / lead-admin, each gated to what they should touch.
- **Hardware-backed sign-in** — staff sign in by scanning a QR + their phone's device-unlock. No
  passwords, no pasted keys.
- **Branding** — each org themes its own check-in screen.
- **Offline-safe** — the dashboard and check-in keep working through a dropped connection and sync
  when it's back (a co-presence that physically happened shouldn't be lost to a flaky signal).
- **Downloadable, verifiable receipts** — attendance you can hand someone and they can independently check.

The hook in one line: **"Proof people were actually there — browser-based, no app, tamper-evident,
and it works even when the wifi doesn't."**

## ⚠️ HONESTY GUARDRAILS — non-negotiable, this is a brand value
The Captain is deliberate that IRLid never oversells. Please hold these exactly:
- Say **"hardware-backed" / "device-unlock (biometric or passcode)"** — NOT "biometric" alone
  (WebAuthn user-verification can be a fingerprint *or* a PIN; we can't claim it was a face).
- Say **"tamper-evident"**, NOT "tamper-proof". Say **"signed record" / "strong evidence"**, NOT "proof"
  in the absolute sense. Co-presence is a **good-faith** guarantee, not a metaphysical certainty.
- No invented metrics, no fake testimonials, no "military-grade". The records are **immutable** (we never
  retroactively rewrite a receipt) — that transparency is itself a selling point; lean into it.
The honesty *is* the pitch. The audience IRLid wins is the one that's tired of being lied to.
